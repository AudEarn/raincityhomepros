Why Seattle Homeowners Need Regular Vent Cleaning

Dryer vents accumulate lint, debris, and moisture that create serious hazards:

  • Fire Prevention: 15,000+ house fires per year are caused by clogged dryer vents

Recommendation: Clean dryer vents every 12 months, or every 6 months for large families.

Frequently Asked Questions About Vent Cleaning in Seattle

How often should I clean my dryer vent in Seattle?
We recommend every 12 months for most households, or every 6 months if you have a large family or do laundry frequently. Seattle's wet climate makes regular cleaning especially important to prevent mold.
What are signs my dryer vent needs cleaning?
Warning signs include: clothes taking longer to dry, dryer feeling hot to the touch, burning smell, visible lint around the dryer, and the outside vent flap not opening properly.
Can I clean my dryer vent myself?
While you can clean the lint trap yourself, professional cleaning is recommended for the entire vent system. Professionals have specialized tools to remove buildup deep in the vent and inspect for damage or code violations.
How long does vent cleaning take?
Most residential dryer vent cleanings take 1-2 hours. More complex systems with long runs or multiple stories may take 2-3 hours.
Is vent cleaning covered by homeowners insurance?
Routine cleaning is typically not covered, but damage caused by neglected vent maintenance may not be covered either. Regular cleaning is considered preventive maintenance and can help avoid insurance claims.
What's the difference between dryer vent and air duct cleaning?
Dryer vent cleaning focuses on the exhaust system that removes hot air and lint from your dryer. Air duct cleaning addresses your home's HVAC ductwork. Both are important for safety and air quality.
Do vent cleaning companies in Seattle offer same-day service?
Many Seattle vent cleaning contractors offer same-day or next-day service, especially for emergency situations. Call ahead to check availability.

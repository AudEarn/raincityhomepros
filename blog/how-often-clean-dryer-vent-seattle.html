How Often Should You Clean Your Dryer Vent in Seattle?

📅 May 11, 2026🕑 6 min read🏷 Vent Cleaning

Home › Blog › How Often to Clean Dryer Vents

If you live in Seattle, your dryer vent deserves more attention than most homeowners realize. The Pacific Northwest's damp, humid climate creates conditions where lint accumulates faster than in drier regions — and clogged dryer vents are the #1 cause of residential dryer fires nationwide.

The Short Answer: Once a Year (Minimum)

The National Fire Protection Association (NFPA) recommends cleaning your dryer vent at least once per year. However, Seattle homeowners should consider cleaning every 6–9 months due to higher average humidity levels that cause lint to clump and stick to duct walls more aggressively.

💡 Seattle Tip: If your dryer takes more than one cycle to fully dry a normal load of laundry, your vent is already partially blocked and needs cleaning now.

Key Factors That Affect How Often You Should Clean

1. Household Size

A single person doing 3–4 loads per week generates far less lint than a family of five doing 10+ loads. More laundry = more lint = more frequent cleaning. Large households in Seattle should clean every 6 months.

2. Vent Length and Configuration

Longer vents with multiple bends — common in Seattle's older craftsman homes and downtown condos — trap lint more easily. Vents over 25 feet or with more than two 90-degree elbows should be cleaned twice a year.

3. Pet Ownership

Pet hair and dander dramatically increase lint levels in dryer vents. Dog and cat owners in Seattle should plan on cleaning every 6 months at minimum, regardless of household size.

4. Vent Material

Flexible plastic or foil accordion-style venting (still found in many Seattle homes built before 2000) traps lint in its ridges. If you have this type, it should ideally be replaced with smooth rigid metal ducting — and cleaned more often in the meantime.

Warning Signs Your Dryer Vent Needs Cleaning Now

  • Clothes take 2+ cycles to fully dry
  • The dryer exterior feels unusually hot to the touch
  • A burning or musty smell when the dryer is running
  • The laundry room feels more humid than usual
  • Visible lint around the dryer's exterior exhaust vent
  • The dryer automatically shuts off before the cycle ends
⚠️ Fire Risk: The U.S. Consumer Product Safety Commission estimates clogged dryer vents cause approximately 15,000 residential fires per year. In Seattle, where homes are often older and vents longer, regular cleaning is essential safety maintenance.

What Does Dryer Vent Cleaning Cost in Seattle?

Professional dryer vent cleaning in Seattle typically costs $89–$175 for a standard residential job. Longer vents, roof exits, or severely clogged systems may cost $150–$250. Most NADCA-certified technicians complete the job in 45–90 minutes.

DIY vs. Professional Cleaning

You can purchase a dryer vent brush kit at any Seattle hardware store for $25–$40 and clean the duct yourself — this is reasonable for short, straight vents under 8 feet. But for longer vents, multiple bends, roof exits, or if you haven't cleaned in 2+ years, a professional is strongly recommended. They use high-powered rotary brushes and vacuum systems that fully remove debris rather than just pushing it further in.

How to Find a Trusted Dryer Vent Cleaner in Seattle

Look for technicians who are NADCA certified (National Air Duct Cleaners Association) or hold a CSIA certification. These credentials ensure the technician follows industry safety standards and uses appropriate equipment.

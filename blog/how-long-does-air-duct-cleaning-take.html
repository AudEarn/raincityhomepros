How Long Does Air Duct Cleaning Take?

📅 May 11, 2026🕑 6 min read🏷 Vent Cleaning

Home › Blog › How Long Does Air Duct Cleaning Take

If you're booking air duct cleaning, you need to know how long to block off your schedule. The short answer: most residential air duct cleaning jobs take 2 to 4 hours. But it depends on your home's size, the number of vents, and how long since the last cleaning. Here's the full breakdown.

How Long Does Air Duct Cleaning Take?

Home SizeTypical TimeVents
Small home / condo (under 1,000 sq ft)1.5 – 2.5 hours6–10 vents
Medium home (1,000–2,500 sq ft)2.5 – 4 hours10–20 vents
Large home (2,500–4,000 sq ft)3.5 – 5 hours20–30 vents
Very large home / dual-system (4,000+ sq ft)5 – 8 hours30+ vents
Dryer vent only45 – 90 min1 duct

What Happens During the Cleaning (Step by Step)

1. Setup and Inspection (15–30 min)

The technician arrives and connects a large negative-pressure vacuum unit (often truck-mounted) to your main duct line via a service port. They inspect accessible ductwork and take photos of current conditions. This is also when they identify any special situations like mold, debris blockages, or damaged ductwork.

2. Negative Pressure Vacuum (Throughout)

The vacuum runs continuously during the entire cleaning to create negative pressure inside the duct system, pulling dislodged debris toward a collection point rather than letting it escape into your home.

3. Cleaning Each Vent (1–5 min per vent)

The technician uses a powered rotary brush (or compressed air whip) to agitate debris from duct walls at each register. They seal off other vents to maintain suction direction. A typical 15-vent home has about 45–75 minutes of active brushing work.

4. Main Trunk Lines (15–30 min)

The main supply and return trunk lines connecting to the air handler are brushed and vacuumed separately. These are larger ducts and take longer per linear foot.

5. Air Handler and Coil Inspection (15 min)

A thorough job includes inspecting (and optionally cleaning) the blower, evaporator coil, and drain pan. These components accumulate biological growth and affect system efficiency.

6. Post-Cleaning Inspection and Reassembly (15–20 min)

The technician reassembles vent covers, removes equipment, does a final visual check, and provides a job report. Some companies include before/after photos.

💡 Plan Ahead: Schedule air duct cleaning for a weekday morning if possible. You'll want to be home during the 2–4 hour window, and starting early ensures everything is done by afternoon.

What Makes a Job Take Longer?

  • Heavy buildup from years without cleaning (adds 30–60 min)
  • Mold treatment required (adds 30–60 min plus drying time)
  • Flexible duct sections that need special care
  • Attic or crawl-space ductwork with difficult access
  • Older homes with non-standard duct configurations (common in Seattle)

Red Flags: When the Job Is Too Fast

Be suspicious if a company claims they can clean your whole home's ducts in under an hour. Proper cleaning with real equipment simply cannot be done that quickly for a standard home. Jobs that take less than 90 minutes for a medium or large home are almost always cutting corners — running minimal equipment and not properly cleaning each branch and vent.

⚠️ Too Fast = Not Clean: If a company shows up with a portable shop-vacuum and finishes a 2,000 sq ft home in 45 minutes, they did not clean your ducts — they just moved through quickly and charged you anyway.

Air Duct Cleaning Seattle: Cost, What's Included & How to Find a Pro

📅 May 11, 2026🕑 7 min read🏷 Vent Cleaning

Home › Blog › Air Duct Cleaning Seattle

Air duct cleaning in Seattle is one of the most impactful home maintenance investments you can make — particularly in a climate that makes mold, dust, and damp-related air quality issues more common than in drier parts of the country. This guide covers what to expect, what it costs, and how to find a reputable company near you.

Air Duct Cleaning in Seattle: What's Different About the PNW

Seattle homeowners face a specific set of air quality challenges that make duct cleaning more valuable here than in most U.S. cities:

  • Mold risk: High humidity and rain from October through May create ideal conditions for mold growth in ductwork, especially in homes with any moisture intrusion history
  • Older housing stock: Much of Seattle's housing was built before 1980, with duct systems that may have never been professionally cleaned
  • Wood heat and fireplace use: Common in Seattle homes, these create additional particulate contamination in air systems
  • Forest fire smoke: Increasing wildfire activity in eastern Washington sends smoke into Seattle every summer, contaminating duct systems

How to Tell If Your Seattle Home Needs Duct Cleaning

Schedule a professional inspection or cleaning if any of the following apply:

  • You've lived in the home more than 3 years without having ducts cleaned
  • You notice musty, stale, or dusty odors when the HVAC runs
  • Allergy or respiratory symptoms are worse indoors than outside
  • You see visible debris or black staining inside vent openings
  • The home has had any water infiltration, flooding, or roof leaks
  • You have pets, or you recently moved into a home that had pets
  • Recent renovation or construction work was done in the home
💡 Seattle-Specific Tip: If you own a craftsman home, Tudor, or any pre-1970 Seattle property, have your ducts inspected regardless of symptoms. These homes often have original duct systems that have never been professionally serviced.

What a Professional Air Duct Cleaning Includes

A proper cleaning by a NADCA-certified Seattle contractor should include:

  • Negative-pressure vacuum attached to main duct trunk
  • Rotary brush cleaning of all supply and return branches
  • Cleaning of all registers and grilles
  • Inspection of air handler, blower compartment, and coils
  • Before and after photos or video documentation
  • Written report of findings

Questions to Ask Before Hiring

Before booking any Seattle air duct cleaning company, ask these questions:

  • Are you NADCA certified? (Ask for the certification number)
  • Do you use truck-mounted or large commercial vacuum equipment?
  • What is included in the quoted price — just the ducts, or also the air handler?
  • Do you provide before and after photos?
  • Are you licensed with Washington State L&I?

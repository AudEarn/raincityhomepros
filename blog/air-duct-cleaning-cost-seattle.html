📅 May 11, 2026🕑 7 min read🏷 Vent Cleaning

One of the most common questions Seattle homeowners ask before booking a vent cleaning is: "How much is this going to cost?" The answer depends on several factors, but this guide gives you the real 2026 price ranges so you can budget accurately and spot any pricing red flags.

What Factors Drive the Price Up or Down?

Number of Vents and Square Footage

Most companies price air duct cleaning per vent or per square foot. A larger Seattle home with more bedrooms, bathrooms, and return air vents will naturally cost more. Always get a quote that specifies whether the price is per-vent or whole-house.

Vent Accessibility and Configuration

Vents located in crawl spaces, attics, or with difficult access points cost more to clean. Seattle's older homes — many built before 1960 — often have non-standard duct configurations that require extra labor.

Degree of Contamination

If you've never cleaned your ducts, or the home had water damage, mold, or heavy pet occupation, expect to pay at the higher end of the range. Severely contaminated systems may require sanitizing treatments on top of the standard cleaning.

Company Certification and Equipment

NADCA-certified companies in Seattle invest in truck-mounted negative pressure vacuum systems and rotary brush equipment — the gold standard for thorough cleaning. These companies typically charge $50–$150 more than uncertified competitors, but the work is genuinely better.

💡 Pro Tip: Always ask whether the quote covers the entire system (supply and return ducts) or just the main trunk lines. Partial cleaning is less effective and some companies use this to quote artificially low prices.

Vent Cleaning Scams to Watch Out For in Seattle

Unfortunately, vent cleaning has a history of scam operations that advertise unrealistically low prices to get in the door. Here are the red flags:

  • $49 whole-house duct cleaning ads: Legitimate whole-house cleaning cannot be done for this price. This is almost always a bait-and-switch.
  • Pressure to add expensive upsells: Some companies claim to find mold in every home and pressure homeowners to pay $500+ for chemical treatments.
  • No NADCA or business license: Always verify credentials before booking.
  • Cash-only payment: Legitimate companies accept credit cards and provide written receipts.
⚠️ Seattle BBB Warning: The Better Business Bureau of Seattle regularly lists duct cleaning as one of the most common home service scam categories. Verify any contractor through the BBB website or our vetted directory before booking.

How to Get the Best Price in Seattle

  • Get 3 quotes minimum — prices vary widely between companies
  • Bundle dryer vent and air duct cleaning for a package discount (typically 15–25% off)
  • Schedule in off-peak months (late spring or early fall) for better availability and occasional promotions
  • Ask about military, senior, or first-responder discounts — many Seattle contractors offer them

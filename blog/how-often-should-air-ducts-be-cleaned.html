How Often Should Air Ducts Be Cleaned?

📅 May 11, 2026🕑 6 min read🏷 Vent Cleaning

Home › Blog › How Often Should Air Ducts Be Cleaned

Most HVAC professionals recommend cleaning your air ducts every 3 to 5 years — but that's a wide range. Whether you need cleaning on the shorter end of that window or can wait longer depends on factors specific to your home. Here's exactly how to figure out the right schedule for you.

How Often Should Air Ducts Be Cleaned? The Standard Guidelines

The National Air Duct Cleaners Association (NADCA) recommends cleaning every 3 to 5 years under normal conditions. The EPA notes that cleaning is beneficial when there is visible mold growth, evidence of pests, or excessive dust and debris. However, Seattle's unique climate means many homes need cleaning more frequently than the national average.

💡 NADCA Recommendation: Clean your air ducts every 3–5 years under normal conditions, or more frequently if any of the trigger conditions below apply.

Clean More Frequently If You Have...

Pets

Pet dander and fur circulate through your HVAC system constantly. Homes with one or more pets should clean ducts every 2–3 years, and the HVAC filter should be changed monthly.

Anyone With Allergies or Asthma

Dirty ducts recirculate allergens, pollen, and irritants every time your heat or AC runs. For households with allergy or asthma sufferers, cleaning every 2–3 years — or when symptoms worsen — is recommended.

A Newly Purchased Home

If you don't know the duct cleaning history of a home you just bought, have it inspected immediately. Previous owners' pets, smoking, or renovation dust may have left significant contamination in the system.

Recent Renovation or Construction

Construction and drywall dust is extremely fine and coats duct walls thoroughly. Always clean ducts after any significant home renovation, regardless of how recently the last cleaning was.

Water Damage or Mold History

If your home has experienced flooding or water infiltration, have ducts inspected for mold immediately. Mold in HVAC ducts spreads spores to every room every time the system runs.

You're in Seattle (Seriously)

Seattle's consistently damp, cloudy climate creates conditions where moisture enters duct systems more easily than in dry climates. This accelerates mold and mildew growth inside ducts. Seattle homeowners should lean toward the 3-year end of the schedule rather than waiting 5 years.

Signs Your Air Ducts Need Cleaning Regardless of Schedule

  • Visible dust blowing from vents when the HVAC turns on
  • Musty or stale odor when the heat or AC runs
  • Increasing dust accumulation on furniture between cleanings
  • Unexplained rise in allergy symptoms indoors
  • Uneven airflow between rooms
  • You can see debris when you remove a vent cover and shine a flashlight inside
⚠️ Mold Warning: If you see black or green staining inside your duct openings or on vent covers, do not run your HVAC system. Call a NADCA-certified professional immediately for inspection and remediation.

Does Air Duct Cleaning Actually Improve Air Quality?

Yes, when done properly. The EPA and NADCA both confirm that duct cleaning removes accumulated dust, allergens, pet dander, mold spores, and other contaminants from the air distribution system. The key word is "properly" — cleaning must use negative-pressure vacuum equipment to actually remove debris rather than just disturb and redistribute it.

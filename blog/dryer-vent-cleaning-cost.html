Dryer Vent Cleaning Cost: What to Expect in 2026

📅 May 11, 2026🕑 7 min read🏷 Vent Cleaning

Wondering how much dryer vent cleaning costs? You're not alone — it's one of the most searched home maintenance questions in the country. The short answer: most homeowners pay between $89 and $175 for a professional dryer vent cleaning. But there's more to the story, especially if you're in Seattle.

What Factors Affect the Price?

Vent Length

The longer your dryer vent, the more time and equipment the technician needs. Standard vents are 10–15 feet. Anything over 25 feet typically adds $30–$75 to the bill.

Number of Bends or Elbows

Each 90-degree elbow adds resistance and lint-trapping points. Vents with 3+ elbows (common in Seattle townhomes and condos) cost more to clean properly and may require specialized tools.

Exit Point

Ground-level exits are easiest and cheapest. Roof exits or soffit exits require ladder access and extra time — expect to add $40–$80.

How Clogged It Is

A vent cleaned annually costs less to service than one neglected for 3+ years. Heavy lint buildup may require multiple passes and longer job time.

Company Certification

NADCA-certified (National Air Duct Cleaners Association) companies invest in proper vacuum and rotary brush equipment. They charge a bit more than uncertified competitors, but the results are significantly better and safer.

💡 Seattle Tip: Seattle's humid climate causes lint to compact more tightly than in dry climates. Budget for the mid-to-upper range if you haven't cleaned in over a year.

Is Dryer Vent Cleaning Worth the Cost?

Absolutely. A clogged dryer vent forces your dryer to work harder, using more electricity per load. The U.S. Department of Energy estimates that a clogged vent can add $18–$24 per month to your energy bill. A $130 cleaning pays for itself in 5–7 months. It also eliminates a major fire risk: clogged dryer vents cause approximately 15,000 house fires per year in the U.S.

Can I Clean My Dryer Vent Myself?

You can buy a dryer vent cleaning brush kit for $20–$40 at any hardware store and clean short, straight vents yourself. This works fine for vents under 8 feet with no bends. For anything longer, with multiple elbows, or if you haven't cleaned in 2+ years, hire a professional. Improper DIY cleaning can push clogs deeper into the duct or damage flexible vinyl ducts.

⚠️ Warning: If your dryer smells like burning while running, or if clothes take 2+ cycles to dry, stop using the dryer until the vent is professionally inspected. These are signs of dangerous lint buildup.

How Often Should You Schedule Dryer Vent Cleaning?

Most households: once per year. Large families, pet owners, or anyone with a vent longer than 15 feet: every 6 months. Seattle homeowners in particular should lean toward the 6-month schedule due to the damp climate. Regular cleaning keeps costs low — a lightly-clogged vent takes less time to clean and costs less than one heavily compacted with lint.
